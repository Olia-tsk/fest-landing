Lector:

Alhaj Hasan Adnan,

TUSUR Researcher

Drones: between joy and risk. What to expect in the nearest future?

Drones are becoming smaller, smarter and cheaper to manufacture, do you think that they have reached a level at which we can consider them as a threat?
How we can deal with this threat and using which security systems? With all the advancing technology, there is no fully effective defense system, where the type and extent of mitigation techniques depend on the environment and situation. Besides the effectiveness issue there is the fact that the drones technology itself is not standing still. The counter-drone market will therefore have to constantly respond to new advances in UAV's technology. There are no standards for ensuring the performance and reliability of the C-drone system so they might present a public safety threat like interference with emergency radio communications, or like missing the intended target by a kinetic system.
We will discuss all this with you and learn more about the drones and the counter-drone security systems. Also, we will talk about the actual projects relating to the drone protection and protection from them in TUSUR university.

Engineers saving lives or Current trends in Bioengineering

Do you know that it is possible to print human tissues and organs and even the human genome? TUSUR University scientists are now being developing a human genome printer. It's no secret that when working with genes, you need to work with very small sizes. For example, a dispenser is also currently being developed that can work with nanometer sizes. In addition, with the rest of the world we are fighting cancer and our researchers can destroy the tumor cells using the high temperatures. And this is far from being the only thing that our research teams do in the field of biomedicine. Currently, TUSUR is working with a large number of different biomedical projects, which you can learn more about at the upcoming event.
